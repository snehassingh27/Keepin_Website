How It Works

Four steps. No learning curve.

Anyone can start in minutes — no barcodes, no setup, no technical knowledge needed.

Step 1

Point your camera

As you put something away, open Keepin and point your camera. The AI identifies the item instantly — no typing needed.

Step 2

Keepin logs it

The app records what the item is, where you put it, and when — building a memory map of your whole space automatically.

Step 3

Just ask

Type or speak: "Where are my keys?" Keepin replies instantly. Ask from anywhere — couch, work, or via Alexa.

Step 4

Virtually rummage

Browse a drawer or shelf on screen before getting up. Scroll through spaces without physically digging through them.

FAQ

Common questions.

Everything you need to know before downloading.

How does Keepin identify items without barcodes?
Keepin uses patent-pending computer vision AI that identifies objects directly from your phone's camera — the way a person would recognise them visually. Point your camera at an item as you put it away and the AI identifies it automatically. No barcodes, stickers, or manual input needed.
Do I need to buy the special containers or tags?
No. Keepin works completely without any special containers or tags. The optional QR/NFC stickers and stackable containers are available if you want to speed things up — but they're entirely optional and priced like any container at a regular retailer.
Can multiple family members use the same account?
Yes. With Personal Pro, up to 5 household members share the same space. If your partner puts something away, everyone can find it — through the app or by asking Alexa or Google Home.
Does it work with Alexa or Google Home?
Yes. Keepin integrates with Amazon Alexa and Google Home so you can ask questions out loud and get instant spoken answers from anywhere in your home.
Is my data private and secure?
Yes. Everything is protected with advanced encryption. Your data is never shared with third parties, and you control exactly who in your household has access. Nothing leaves your account without your explicit permission.
When will the app be fully available?
Keepin is currently in beta. Early access is available now on iOS, with Android coming soon. Full public launch is planned for this year following beta testing and manufacturing of the optional container products.
